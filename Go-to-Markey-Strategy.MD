## **Analysis and Recommendations for P3-Lending-Protocol: A Pre-VC Pitch Review**

### **1. Introduction: The Vision and the Multi-Trillion Dollar Opportunity**

The P3-Lending-Protocol, a collaborative research project from MIT Emerging Talent Group 12, is centered on a powerful and socially impactful mission: leveraging blockchain-based credit solutions to provide financial services to migrants and refugees [draft_document]. This demographic often lacks the traditional credit histories or formal banking access required to obtain loans and insurance [draft_document]. By addressing this gap, the project has the potential to create significant positive social change while tapping into a vast and underserved market [draft_document].

The total addressable market (TAM) for this venture is staggering [draft_document]. Globally, there are **1.3 billion unbanked adults**, with over half concentrated in just eight emerging economies [[1]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/goldfinch-borrower-lend-east-defaults-says-warbler-labs/)[[2]](https://devpuk.co.uk/decentralized-finance-the-financial-revolution-africa-cannot-afford-to-miss/) . In the United States alone, an estimated **45 million people are "credit invisible"** or "unscoreable" by traditional metrics, a condition that disproportionately affects minority populations [[3]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/6440233109458)[[4]](https://www.bitget.com/news/detail/12560603956589)[[5]](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/357601906_Decentralised_finance_and_cryptocurrency_activity_in_Africa) . The global alternative lending market is projected to reach **$884.1 billion by 2029**, while the DeFi market is expected to surge to **$390.47 billion by 2030**, driven largely by the demand for financial inclusion [[6]](https://tokeninsight.com/en/news/real-world-asset-lending-protocol-goldfinch-face-loan-default)[[7]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/rwa-protocol-goldfinch-cuts-ties-with-risk-adviser/)[[8]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/978895)[[9]](https://medium.com/coinmonks/defi-weekly-goldfinch-the-protocol-that-brought-crypto-capital-to-emerging-markets-9c2cd7952796)[[10]](https://www.techinafrica.com/defi-regulations-in-africa-key-challenges/) . This presents a clear, multi-trillion dollar opportunity for a protocol like P3 [draft_document].

The "P3" in the project's name may also allude to a Public-Private Partnership, a model that could be highly effective for a venture with a strong social mandate [draft_document]. To succeed, the protocol must learn from the challenges faced by pioneers in the real-world asset (RWA) and undercollateralized lending space [draft_document]. This report will provide a comprehensive analysis of potential areas for improvement for the P3-Lending-Protocol before it is presented to venture capital investors, focusing on risk management, technical architecture, and legal strategy informed by real-world case studies [draft_document].

### **2. Learning from Precedent: Goldfinch, Centrifuge, and the RWA Frontier**

Before detailing recommendations for P3, it is crucial to analyze the experiences of protocols that have ventured into lending against real-world assets, often in emerging markets [[1]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/goldfinch-borrower-lend-east-defaults-says-warbler-labs/) .

#### **The Goldfinch Protocol's Cautionary Tale**

Goldfinch was designed to provide undercollateralized loans to businesses, having loaned over $170 million through a two-tranche system of junior "Backers" and senior "Liquidity Providers" [[11]](https://www.rapidinnovation.io/post/defi-in-insurance) . Despite its innovative model, Goldfinch has faced at least three major loan defaults totaling nearly $18 million, providing invaluable, if difficult, lessons for the entire sector [[1]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/goldfinch-borrower-lend-east-defaults-says-warbler-labs/) .

*   **Lend East ($5.9 Million Default):** This default was attributed by the community to a "poorly executed" initial credit assessment and a lack of communication [[12]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/2024-04-07-decentralized-finance-protocol-goldfinch-suffers-after-major-borrower-defaults-6439680527202) . The protocol's model, which relies on a decentralized network of auditors for underwriting, came under heavy criticism, with investors accusing the auditors of performing a subpar job [[1]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/goldfinch-borrower-lend-east-defaults-says-warbler-labs/)[[12]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/2024-04-07-decentralized-finance-protocol-goldfinch-suffers-after-major-borrower-defaults-6439680527202)[[3]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/6440233109458) . This case highlights the potential weakness of a purely decentralized underwriting process without sufficient checks and balances [draft_document].
*   **Tugende ($5 Million Default):** The default of this Kenyan asset-financing company was caused by a breach of the loan agreement; Tugende Kenya made an unauthorized $1.9 million loan to its Ugandan parent company [[8]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/978895)[[13]](https://gov.goldfinch.finance/t/update-on-the-tugende-kenya-facility/1809)[[14]](https://rwa.xyz/blog/case-study-goldfinch-distressed-debt)[[15]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/african-motorbike-finance-company-defaults-on-loan/) . This violation, combined with macroeconomic pressures like inflation, led to the default [[16]](https://wazoplus.com/article/ugandas-tugende-takes-steps-to-repay-5m-goldfinch-loan-after-default--54b5101b)[[14]](https://rwa.xyz/blog/case-study-goldfinch-distressed-debt) . This demonstrates the critical need for robust legal covenants and monitoring mechanisms that can track off-chain business activities [draft_document].
*   **Stratos ($7 Million Loss):** A loan to a US-based credit fund soured after its investments in real estate technology and digital assets "did not meet expectations" [[17]](https://www.aicoin.com/en/article/369376)[[18]](https://www.bitget.com/news/detail/12560603956577)[[19]](https://tokeninsight.com/en/news/rwa-lending-protocol-goldfinch-faces-7m-bad-loan) . Notably, Warbler Labs, the core entity behind Goldfinch, had to step in and backstop the losses for protocol users, taking on the full risk of recovery [[1]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/goldfinch-borrower-lend-east-defaults-says-warbler-labs/)[[3]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/6440233109458) . This action, while protecting lenders, centralized the risk and called into question the protocol's decentralized nature [[20]](https://gov.goldfinch.finance/t/gip-62-allocate-gfi-token-from-treasury-for-principal-loan-losses/1965?page=2) .

These events underscore the immense challenges of underwriting, monitoring, and enforcing loans in the real world [draft_document]. The recovery process often involves hiring expensive "global tier 1 law firms" and navigating complex, time-consuming off-chain legal systems, with uncertain outcomes [[15]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/african-motorbike-finance-company-defaults-on-loan/)[[21]](https://research.nansen.ai/articles/goldfinch-a-defi-credit-protocol)[[22]](https://gov.goldfinch.finance/t/gip-41-appoint-warbler-labs-to-act-on-behalf-of-the-goldfinch-community-in-the-resolution-of-tugende-kenya-s-covenant-breach/1566) . P3 must build its protocol with these realities in mind [[11]](https://www.rapidinnovation.io/post/defi-in-insurance) .

#### **The Centrifuge Model: Tokenizing Real-World Assets**

Centrifuge offers another model, allowing real-world assets like invoices and mortgages to be tokenized as NFTs and used as collateral in its Tinlake dApp . This enables asset originators to access DeFi liquidity, also using a two-tranche investment model (`DROP` for senior, `TIN` for junior) to cater to different risk appetites [[23]](https://defiguide.org/dai-makerdao/) . Centrifuge's integration with MakerDAO to allow RWAs as collateral for the DAI stablecoin was a landmark step in diversifying DeFi collateral beyond crypto-native assets [[24]](https://makerdao.com/whitepaper/DaiDec17WP.pdf) .

### **3. Smart Contract and Technical Excellence: The Foundation of Trust**

For any DeFi protocol, but especially one handling the funds of a vulnerable population, the security, efficiency, and technical architecture are paramount [draft_document].

#### **Security First: Building an Unbreachable Fortress**

*   **Leverage Audited Libraries:** It is crucial to use well-tested and widely trusted libraries like OpenZeppelin for common functionalities to reduce the risk of introducing vulnerabilities [draft_document].
*   **Comprehensive Security Audits:** Before any public deployment, the smart contracts must undergo multiple independent security audits from reputable firms [draft_document]. This is a non-negotiable step that provides critical external validation [draft_document].
*   **Proactive Vulnerability Detection:** Testing should proactively identify and mitigate common smart contract vulnerabilities such as reentrancy attacks, integer overflows, and front-running [draft_document].
*   **On-Chain Monitoring and Legal Integration:** Smart contracts should not only monitor loan covenants but also be designed to facilitate legal recourse [draft_document, 7, 8, 37]. For instance, contracts can be structured to act as an escrow, giving the protocol the unilateral ability to take possession of digital collateral upon a verified default, thereby establishing "control" under emerging legal frameworks like the UCC Article 12 [[25]](https://www.osl.com/hk-en/academy/article/defi-protocols-and-ai-building-smarter-more-efficient-finance-solutions) .

#### **Bridging the Gap: Decentralized Oracles for Off-Chain Data**

To use alternative data for credit scoring, P3 will need a robust and secure oracle infrastructure to bring this off-chain information on-chain [draft_document]. A custom decentralized oracle network is essential [[15]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/african-motorbike-finance-company-defaults-on-loan/) .

*   **Technical Architecture:**
    *   **Multiple, Independent Nodes:** Data must be sourced from numerous, geographically distributed nodes to prevent a single point of failure and ensure decentralization .
    *   **Data Aggregation:** The oracle network should aggregate data from various sources, using a consensus mechanism to validate accuracy before delivering it to the smart contract [[15]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/african-motorbike-finance-company-defaults-on-loan/)[[26]](https://docs.truefi.io/faq/truefi-protocol/tru-token/stake) .
    *   **Incentive Mechanisms:** Node operators must be required to stake collateral (e.g., the P3 token), which can be slashed for providing inaccurate data, creating a strong "skin-in-the-game" incentive for honesty [[26]](https://docs.truefi.io/faq/truefi-protocol/tru-token/stake) .
*   **Security Best Practices:**
    *   **Staleness Checks & Deviation Limits:** Implement thresholds for data freshness (e.g., 15-60 minutes) and set limits on how much data can change in a single update (e.g., 5-10%) to prevent manipulation or reliance on outdated information .
    *   **Circuit Breakers:** Integrate emergency mechanisms that can pause protocol operations if data feeds become unreliable or show signs of manipulation .
    *   **Defense in Depth:** Utilize multiple layers of security and independent oracle sources (e.g., Chainlink alongside a custom solution) to provide redundancy and resilience against attacks [[27]](https://www.hadu.app/articles/3NkWZQdn8Li9Kymk4)[[28]](https://www.coinspeaker.com/guides/introduction-to-truefi-tru-defis-leading-credit-protocol/) .

#### **Gas Optimization and Code Clarity**

*   **Minimize On-Chain Data:** Keep as much data off-chain as possible, only storing essential information like loan status and ownership on the blockchain to save gas fees, which is critical for a financially sensitive user base [draft_document].
*   **Efficient Code:** Use efficient data types (`bytes32`), variable packing, and `memory` for temporary calculations to minimize costly storage writes [draft_document].
*   **Modular and Documented Code:** A clean, well-commented, and modular codebase is crucial for attracting talent, facilitating audits, and enabling future development [draft_document].

### **4. Sustainable Economics and Governance: The Long-Term Vision**

A well-designed economic and governance model is crucial for long-term sustainability [draft_document]. This is where P3 can demonstrate it has learned from the challenges faced by its predecessors [draft_document].

#### **Lending Protocol Mechanics: A Resilient Framework**

*   **Innovative Collateralization and Credit Assessment:** Given that the target users may lack traditional collateral, a multi-pronged approach to credit assessment is essential [draft_document].
    *   **Professionalized Underwriting (Maple Finance Model):** The Lend East default demonstrates the risk of relying solely on a decentralized and potentially uncoordinated group of auditors [[12]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/2024-04-07-decentralized-finance-protocol-goldfinch-suffers-after-major-borrower-defaults-6439680527202) . P3 should consider a hybrid model inspired by Maple Finance, which utilizes professional "Pool Delegates" [[1]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/goldfinch-borrower-lend-east-defaults-says-warbler-labs/)[[8]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/978895)[[29]](https://www.emerald.com/insight/content/doi/10.1108/s1569-37592022000109a001/full/html) . These are experienced credit professionals responsible for conducting rigorous due diligence and negotiating loan terms off-chain before formalizing them on-chain [[1]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/goldfinch-borrower-lend-east-defaults-says-warbler-labs/)[[7]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/rwa-protocol-goldfinch-cuts-ties-with-risk-adviser/)[[10]](https://www.techinafrica.com/defi-regulations-in-africa-key-challenges/) . This adds a layer of professional accountability that is attractive to institutional capital [[6]](https://tokeninsight.com/en/news/real-world-asset-lending-protocol-goldfinch-face-loan-default) .
    *   **AI-Powered Credit Scoring with Alternative Data:** P3 can implement sophisticated credit scoring by leveraging alternative data sources. However, it must be mindful of potential pitfalls like data inaccuracy and unintended biases [[3]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/6440233109458)[[30]](https://www.nps.gov/hdp/scripts/pannellum/pannellum.htm?config=/\/cdn.wsscript.com/gov/article/truefi.txt) . Key data points include:
        *   **Utility and Bill Payments:** Consistent payment history for rent, mobile phone bills, and cable TV [[3]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/6440233109458)[[5]](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/357601906_Decentralised_finance_and_cryptocurrency_activity_in_Africa) .
        *   **Bank Account & Payments Data:** Analysis of bank account cash flow and real-time payment (RTP) system usage [[3]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/6440233109458)[[4]](https://www.bitget.com/news/detail/12560603956589)[[16]](https://wazoplus.com/article/ugandas-tugende-takes-steps-to-repay-5m-goldfinch-loan-after-default--54b5101b) .
        *   **Digital Footprint:** Data from mobile phone usage (e.g., presence of financial apps) and social media activity can help predict creditworthiness [[6]](https://tokeninsight.com/en/news/real-world-asset-lending-protocol-goldfinch-face-loan-default)[[30]](https://www.nps.gov/hdp/scripts/pannellum/pannellum.htm?config=/\/cdn.wsscript.com/gov/article/truefi.txt) .
        *   **Educational and Occupational Data:** Information about a person's education and employment history [[3]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/6440233109458)[[5]](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/357601906_Decentralised_finance_and_cryptocurrency_activity_in_Africa) .
    *   **Social Collateral and Reputation-as-Collateral:** For users without extensive data histories, P3 can implement models that leverage social trust [draft_document].
        *   **Peer-to-Peer Vouching (Union Protocol Model):** Implement a system where trusted community members can "vouch" for a borrower by staking their own capital [[16]](https://wazoplus.com/article/ugandas-tugende-takes-steps-to-repay-5m-goldfinch-loan-after-default--54b5101b)[[26]](https://docs.truefi.io/faq/truefi-protocol/tru-token/stake) . The borrower's credit line is determined by the total amount vouched, and vouchers absorb losses in a default [[13]](https://gov.goldfinch.finance/t/update-on-the-tugende-kenya-facility/1809)[[27]](https://www.hadu.app/articles/3NkWZQdn8Li9Kymk4)[[15]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/african-motorbike-finance-company-defaults-on-loan/)[[26]](https://docs.truefi.io/faq/truefi-protocol/tru-token/stake) .
        *   **Reputation-as-Collateral (LendOne Model):** Allow users to deposit on-chain social profiles (e.g., Lens, ENS) as collateral, with their reputational value at stake [[31]](https://gov.goldfinch.finance/t/update-2-warbler-labs-and-tugende-have-agreed-in-principle-to-a-restructuring-plan-that-may-result-in-a-material-recovery-for-the-goldfinch-senior-pool/1835)[[32]](https://gov.goldfinch.finance/t/gip-52-tugende-response-plan/1841)[[28]](https://www.coinspeaker.com/guides/introduction-to-truefi-tru-defis-leading-credit-protocol/)[[11]](https://www.rapidinnovation.io/post/defi-in-insurance) .

*   **Default Handling and Loss Mitigation:** A default is a matter of "when," not "if" [draft_document]. P3 must have a multi-layered, transparent plan for handling defaults [draft_document].
    *   **Two-Tiered First-Loss Capital (Maple Finance Model):** Adopt a sophisticated, two-layered approach inspired by Maple Finance [[29]](https://www.emerald.com/insight/content/doi/10.1108/s1569-37592022000109a001/full/html)[[21]](https://research.nansen.ai/articles/goldfinch-a-defi-credit-protocol) .
        1.  **First-Loss Capital:** Require the professional underwriters ("Pool Delegates") to provide a "first-loss" capital pool, strongly aligning their incentives with lenders [[3]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/6440233109458)[[5]](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/357601906_Decentralised_finance_and_cryptocurrency_activity_in_Africa) .
        2.  **Pool Cover (Staking & Slashing):** Implement a system where other participants can stake the native P3 token into a "Pool Cover" fund to provide a second layer of insurance, which is "slashed" to cover losses [[29]](https://www.emerald.com/insight/content/doi/10.1108/s1569-37592022000109a001/full/html)[[21]](https://research.nansen.ai/articles/goldfinch-a-defi-credit-protocol) .
    *   **Web3-Native Enforcement Mechanisms:**
        *   **Reputational Damage (RociFi Model):** Reserve the right to publicly reveal the wallet addresses of defaulted borrowers, damaging their on-chain reputation [[32]](https://gov.goldfinch.finance/t/gip-52-tugende-response-plan/1841)[[33]](https://www.signum.capital/blog/chris-brookins-co-founder-of-rocifi-permissionless-under-collateralized-credit-in-defi/) .
        *   **Functional Restrictions (LendOne Model):** If a user defaults on a loan collateralized by a social profile, the protocol can trigger a "soft ban," blocking interactions on the platform or freezing funds sent to a linked address until the debt is settled [[31]](https://gov.goldfinch.finance/t/update-2-warbler-labs-and-tugende-have-agreed-in-principle-to-a-restructuring-plan-that-may-result-in-a-material-recovery-for-the-goldfinch-senior-pool/1835)[[28]](https://www.coinspeaker.com/guides/introduction-to-truefi-tru-defis-leading-credit-protocol/)[[11]](https://www.rapidinnovation.io/post/defi-in-insurance) .

*   **On-the-Ground Partnerships (JUMO Model):** To effectively reach its target demographic, P3 should pursue partnerships with on-the-ground entities. The collaboration between mobile operator **Orange and fintech JUMO** in Africa is a prime example .
    *   **Partnership Structure:** P3 could partner with local MFIs or mobile network operators who provide the distribution channel and access to customer data (with consent), while P3 provides the AI-driven credit scoring and DeFi liquidity platform .
    *   **Technical Integration:** The partnership would leverage mobile money transaction data to assess creditworthiness and disburse microloans, enabling data-driven, responsible lending [[16]](https://wazoplus.com/article/ugandas-tugende-takes-steps-to-repay-5m-goldfinch-loan-after-default--54b5101b) .
    *   **Navigating Challenges:** This model requires navigating complex and fragmented regulatory landscapes across different jurisdictions, with a focus on consumer protection and data privacy [[31]](https://gov.goldfinch.finance/t/update-2-warbler-labs-and-tugende-have-agreed-in-principle-to-a-restructuring-plan-that-may-result-in-a-material-recovery-for-the-goldfinch-senior-pool/1835)[[32]](https://gov.goldfinch.finance/t/gip-52-tugende-response-plan/1841)[[34]](https://www.antiersolutions.com/blogs/how-is-ai-revolutionizing-defi-lending-platform-development/) .

### **5. Crafting the VC Pitch: Storytelling, Strategy, and Risk Mitigation**

When pitching to VCs, it's essential to present not only a solid technical project but also a compelling vision and a clear, battle-tested path to success [draft_document].

#### **Highlighting the "Double Bottom Line" and TAM**

Start by framing the massive market opportunity: a multi-trillion dollar market of 1.3 billion unbanked individuals and a projected DeFi market size of nearly $400 billion [[1]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/goldfinch-borrower-lend-east-defaults-says-warbler-labs/)[[9]](https://medium.com/coinmonks/defi-weekly-goldfinch-the-protocol-that-brought-crypto-capital-to-emerging-markets-9c2cd7952796)[[10]](https://www.techinafrica.com/defi-regulations-in-africa-key-challenges/)[[2]](https://devpuk.co.uk/decentralized-finance-the-financial-revolution-africa-cannot-afford-to-miss/) . Emphasize the project's potential for both significant financial returns and profound social impact [draft_document].

#### **Demonstrating a Clear Roadmap and Technical Foresight**

Present a realistic roadmap for development, user acquisition, and partnerships [draft_document]. Showcase technical sophistication by detailing the architecture for decentralized oracles, including security measures like staleness checks and circuit breakers [[27]](https://www.hadu.app/articles/3NkWZQdn8Li9Kymk4)[[28]](https://www.coinspeaker.com/guides/introduction-to-truefi-tru-defis-leading-credit-protocol/) .

#### **Addressing Key VC Concerns Proactively**

VCs familiar with DeFi will be acutely aware of past failures and emerging legal risks [draft_document]. Address these concerns head-on by presenting a more sophisticated and resilient model [draft_document].

*   **Scalability and Partnerships:** Discuss how the protocol will scale its user base, highlighting the JUMO-style partnership model as a proven strategy for distribution in emerging markets [[16]](https://wazoplus.com/article/ugandas-tugende-takes-steps-to-repay-5m-goldfinch-loan-after-default--54b5101b) .
*   **Risk Management & Default Strategy:** This will be the most scrutinized part of your pitch [draft_document].
    *   Clearly articulate how your underwriting process is more robust, combining professional "Pool Delegates" (like Maple) with AI models using specific alternative data points (utility payments, mobile usage) and social collateral mechanisms (like Union) [[1]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/goldfinch-borrower-lend-east-defaults-says-warbler-labs/)[[3]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/6440233109458)[[29]](https://www.emerald.com/insight/content/doi/10.1108/s1569-37592022000109a001/full/html)[[5]](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/357601906_Decentralised_finance_and_cryptocurrency_activity_in_Africa)[[26]](https://docs.truefi.io/faq/truefi-protocol/tru-token/stake) .
    *   Present your multi-layered default handling mechanism—combining Delegate-provided first-loss capital with a staked "Pool Cover"—as a core strength that protects lenders [[3]](https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/6440233109458)[[21]](https://research.nansen.ai/articles/goldfinch-a-defi-credit-protocol)[[5]](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/357601906_Decentralised_finance_and_cryptocurrency_activity_in_Africa) .
*   **Regulatory Compliance and Legal Liability:** This is a critical differentiator. Demonstrate a mature, proactive approach to the complex legal landscape [draft_document].
    *   **Developer Liability (The Tornado Cash Precedent):** Acknowledge the recent conviction of a Tornado Cash developer for operating an unlicensed money transmitting business [[17]](https://www.aicoin.com/en/article/369376)[[35]](https://www.bitdeal.net/defi-lending-platform-with-ai-risk-scoring) . Explain that this case, along with actions against Samourai Wallet, shows that developers can be held liable if they knowingly facilitate illicit activity [[25]](https://www.osl.com/hk-en/academy/article/defi-protocols-and-ai-building-smarter-more-efficient-finance-solutions)[[1]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/goldfinch-borrower-lend-east-defaults-says-warbler-labs/) . State that P3's strategy from day one will be to conduct illicit finance risk assessments and implement robust mitigation measures to prevent misuse of the protocol [[36]](https://www.aigisforensics.com/case-studies/defi-recovery) . This turns a major risk into a demonstration of responsibility.
    *   **SEC, CFTC, and OFAC Scrutiny:** Show awareness that the SEC is targeting unregistered securities offerings, the CFTC is regulating DeFi derivatives, and OFAC is actively sanctioning addresses [[6]](https://tokeninsight.com/en/news/real-world-asset-lending-protocol-goldfinch-face-loan-default)[[14]](https://rwa.xyz/blog/case-study-goldfinch-distressed-debt)[[15]](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/african-motorbike-finance-company-defaults-on-loan/) . Position P3 as a protocol built for compliance.
